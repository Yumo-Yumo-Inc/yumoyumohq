# Part II — Spending Identity

A receipt history is a behavioural record. Across weeks of purchases, patterns
appear: when a person shops, how often they buy on discount, how widely they roam
between merchants, how much of their basket is comfort versus necessity. Yumo Yumo
reads these patterns into a **spending identity** — a compact, evidence-backed
portrait a person can recognise.

The identity is built to a strict rule: **every trait maps to one concrete signal in
the receipts.** Nothing is inferred from a personality quiz or a guess. If the data
for a signal is absent, the trait returns empty rather than a fabricated number.

This part proceeds in five steps:

- **Six signals** — the six traits and the single receipt signal behind each, plus
  how class and tribe are composed.
- **The behavioural basis** — the research each trait's concept draws on.
- **Class & tribe** — how the two strongest traits name a class, and how people who
  share a class and a city form a tribe.
- **Wider literature** — the broader reading behind the layer, beyond the per-trait
  basis.
- **Ethics** — the rules that bound the reading: no fabrication, honest empty
  states, and privacy.

As in Part I, this paper names the research a trait draws on but not the cutoff that
scores it. The thresholds are calibrated in production.
