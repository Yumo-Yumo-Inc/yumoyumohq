# Wider literature

Beyond the per-trait basis, the spending-identity layer draws on a broader body of
work — in behavioural economics, the sociology of consumption, research specific to
Turkey, and the lineage of personal data visualisation. This section records that
reading.

## 4.1 Behavioural economics

- **Mullainathan & Shafir — *Scarcity*.** Tunnelling and the bandwidth tax under
  financial pressure; relevant to reading spending in a high-inflation context.
- **Thaler & Sunstein — *Nudge*.** Defaults that quietly favour the chooser's own
  welfare; the stance behind presenting an identity without prescribing behaviour.
- **Ryan & Deci — Self-Determination Theory.** Autonomy, competence, relatedness —
  the three needs that frame how the identity is presented as something a person
  owns.
- **James Clear — *Atomic Habits*.** Identity-based habits: each action a vote for
  the person one is becoming.

## 4.2 Consumer sociology

- **Pierre Bourdieu — *Distinction*.** Habitus and cultural capital; the basket as a
  signal of settled taste.
- **Viviana Zelizer — *The Social Meaning of Money*.** Earmarking: money tagged by
  purpose — gift, care, household.
- **Daniel Miller — *A Theory of Shopping*.** The everyday shop as an act of care for
  the household.
- **Mary Douglas — *The World of Goods*.** Consumption as a system of classification
  and meaning.
- **Thorstein Veblen — *The Theory of the Leisure Class*.** Conspicuous consumption
  and status signalling.

## 4.3 Turkey-specific research

- **Karademir Hazır (2014, *The Sociological Review*)** — cultural capital and food
  choice in Turkey.
- **Rankin, Ergin & Gökşen (2014, *Cultural Sociology*)** — a cultural map of
  Turkey's tastes and distinctions.
- **Mintaş et al. (2025, MDPI)** — Turkish Gen Z spending shifts under inflation,
  including the "recession rush" of buying ahead of price increases.

## 4.4 Data-visualisation lineage

The way the identity is *shown* — organic, person-specific, non-judgmental — draws
on a tradition of personal data visualisation:

- **Giorgia Lupi — Data Humanism.** Embrace complexity, sneak in context, remember
  data is imperfect.
- **Federica Fragapane.** Organic, person-specific data shapes rather than generic
  charts.
- **Nicholas Felton.** A year of personal data read as biography.
- **Mona Chalabi.** A human, non-judgmental tone — data is collected by fallible
  people.
