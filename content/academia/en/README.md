# Academia — The science behind a receipt

A receipt is evidence. It records what a person paid, where, for which goods, at
what time. Yumo Yumo reads two things from that evidence: an estimate of the
**hidden cost** carried over producer cost, and a **spending identity** derived
from how a person shops. This document sets out how both are produced, and on what
they rest.

## Purpose

This paper describes method, not marketing. Each section states what a layer does,
which input it takes, which output it returns, and which source or body of research
grounds it. Where a figure comes from an institution, the institution is named with
its dataset and effective date. Where a reading of behaviour draws on the
literature, the author and the work are cited.

## The two layers

**Hidden cost.** For a line on a receipt, the estimate compares the price paid
against a reference built from official price statistics, producer-to-retail
markups, and category cost composition. The result answers a single question — how
much of this price sits above the cost of producing the good. Part I covers the
model family, the data foundations, the live source registry, and the limits.

**Spending identity.** From a person's receipt history, Yumo Yumo reads six
independent traits, each scored from one concrete signal in the receipts. The two
strongest traits name a class; people who share a class and a city form a tribe.
Part II covers the six signals, the behavioural-science basis for each, how class
and tribe compose, and the ethics that bound the reading.

## Two commitments

**Every figure is sourced.** A value enters the calculation only with a source —
an institution, a reference, an effective date. When a source is missing, the gap
is shown rather than filled with an invented number. The source registry in Part I
is read live from the database, so it reflects what is verified at the moment of
reading.

**Parameters stay in production.** This paper explains what each mechanism does and
which research it draws on. The exact values that operate the mechanisms —
thresholds, weights, coefficients — are calibrated in production and are not
published here. They are engineering decisions, and the paper does not present them
as research.
