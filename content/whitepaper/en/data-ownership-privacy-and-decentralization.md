# Data Ownership, Privacy, and Decentralization

User sovereignty sits at the center of Yumo Yumo’s data model. Spending history, product memory, and price series belong to the user. The system processes that information in order to create clearer guidance, stronger living memory, and anonymized contribution signals that support the open economy. This is implemented through a layered architecture that starts with receipt capture on the device, allows short-lived encrypted processing copies in the system layer, works with structured derivatives for the long-lived product logic, and moves selected summaries and commitments into the open economic rail.

Original receipt images start on the user’s device and can remain there as the user’s personal archive. During verification, encrypted processing copies may temporarily exist in system storage. The system side then holds structured records that power product, merchant, timing, category, and basket analysis. Above that, anonymized derivatives produce collective signals without directly identifying the person behind them. The on-chain layer carries commitments and selected summaries that support contribution, conversion, and open coordination.

The user holds strong operational rights over that data. System-side personal records can be removed in one flow. Structured spending history, product and merchant memory, price series, and contribution summaries can be exported. Selected summaries can be verified against an on-chain ownership trace. Data therefore behaves less like a static pile of records and more like a user-controlled asset that can move with the person who created it.

| Data layer | Where it lives | What it does |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Original receipt image | User device + short-lived encrypted processing | Personal archive, upload input, and verification source |
| Structured receipt record | System layer | Memory, search, basket, and price analysis |
| Anonymized derivative | Aggregate data layer | Collective insight and contribution signals |
| On-chain summary | Open economy layer | Ownership trace, commitments, contribution, coordination |

Decentralization in this frame does more than describe a technical stack. It creates continuity and visibility. Economic rules, emission curves, and contribution logic accumulate on an open rail. As community participation grows, governance becomes more meaningful. Multisig structures and later community processes support the transition from a founder-led early system into a wider ownership circle.

Privacy and openness therefore operate together. The personal layer stays with the user. The collective layer carries anonymized signals. The open economy layer makes economic rules visible without exposing personal financial life. That balance strengthens user trust while also presenting a more credible infrastructure story to investors and partners.
