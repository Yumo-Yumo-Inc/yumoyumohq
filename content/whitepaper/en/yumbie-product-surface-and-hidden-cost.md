# Yumbie, Product Surface, and Hidden Cost

Yumbie is the visible guide layer of Yumo Yumo. It turns financial memory into understandable guidance that meets the user on the day, in the week, and inside the current priority. The tone is warm, while the layer remains deeply structural; merchant memory, price series, approaching bills, recurring baskets, and goal flows all shape which signal Yumbie brings forward.

The product surface is broader than a single screen. The home surface gathers the day’s priorities. The payments surface organizes upcoming and recurring flows. The shopping surface makes frequent products, merchant preference, and price pressure visible. The goals surface connects money movement with intention. The Contribution Hub shows how the user improves the system through tasks, validation, and community participation. All of those surfaces read the same memory layer, so each one becomes sharper as the others grow.

Hidden Cost sits inside this structure as a powerful narrative layer. It helps the user read the pressure inside a paid amount by opening the forces that often sit behind the label: rent, energy, logistics, supply, taxes, and regional pricing stress. The user therefore sees more than a simple price increase; the system explains where the pressure is accumulating and which basket patterns deserve closer attention. Hidden Cost works above the core Proof of Expense engine as an interpretation layer.

| Product surface | Primary role | User impact |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Home | Gather daily priorities | Immediate clarity and direction |
| Payments | Organize upcoming flows | Control and readiness |
| Shopping | Open product, merchant, and price memory | Stronger price awareness |
| Goals | Connect intention with cash movement | Continuity and focus |
| Contribution Hub | Make contribution visible | Shared growth with the network |

Yumbie’s deepest strength is the ability to connect those surfaces. A price increase at the grocery store may be the most important signal for one user, while an approaching rent date or a travel preparation cycle may matter more for another. Yumbie reads patterns in memory and builds that order of importance. That is why the Yumo experience behaves less like an expanded reporting dashboard and more like a guidance system that travels with the rhythm of life.

Hidden Cost also gives price memory a more human language. A changing label is a numerical move. Hidden Cost opens the meaning of that move inside the user’s real day. It shows which inputs carry the pressure, which category is tightening faster, and which basket pattern deserves more attention. In the broader Yumo frame, this becomes one of the most compelling surfaces for sustained user interest.

## Yumbie as Foundation NFT

Yumbie is also the user's persistent digital identity inside Yumo. After the first verified Proof of Expense and a connected wallet, each user mints a Foundation NFT — Yumbie — at gas cost only. The NFT is non-transferable; it cannot be sold or moved between wallets, and it stays with the user as the visible record of their level, mood, and history.

The Foundation form carries the recognizable yellow-receipt silhouette that marks the entry of contribution. As the user grows through level and contribution health, the Yumbie evolves with them. When the user reaches Level 30, the Yumbie transitions from its Foundation form to a Smart Agent. The Smart Agent takes a more formal, letterhead-style presentation that signals established standing inside the system. The evolution is one-way; the underlying NFT remains the same on-chain asset, only its visible expression matures.

This identity layer makes Yumbie more than a guide. The product surface, the contribution rail, and the user's on-chain presence all converge on the same companion. Token design, product experience, and digital identity grow on one connected backbone.
